Projects

iXplore

This web application allows me to pin on a map all the places I want to visit as well as the places I have visited. Then I'm able to check off each place as I go and upload those gorgeous pictures that usually just sit on my camera. I favorite the good ones and soon will allow myself to share a link to the map.

Built with Angular 8 while also integrating Google APIs for login and map. On the backend I used Node.js, MongoDB, and AWS S3 to save all the images. Currently expanding this project so others can create their own map!

On The House

For a Mobile Development course our goal was to create any app of our desire. After long thought and many ideas, usually to far fetched to get done in the limited time, we landed on the idea of On The House. It's a location based raffle, when inside the radius of a participating restaurant/bar the app enters you into their raffle. The app counts how many times you win, lose, raffles entered, and your percentage to win each raffle. The idea was to bring some sense of camaraderie with the people inside the establishment.

Built for iOS with Swift. We used Java and MySQL to handle the raffle operations and to store data. A cron job ran periodically to choose a winner then send a push notification to the winning iPhone. The winner would then be shown a unique screen that could get them a discounted or possibly free item.

Party.sh

When two roommates and I hosted an Oktoberfest party we wanted to have some sort of activity that was constantly happening. So, we wrote a bash script that selected random people and told them what to do (keg stand, spin, take a beerski). The script ran at random intervals and spoke over the music through the speakers using the Mac 'Say' Command. Everyone was anxiously waiting to hear who would be called out next. By far the most memorable project we created.
